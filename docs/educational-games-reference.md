# Educational Games & Activities Reference

This document contains reference games and activities for teachers to use across all subjects.

## Physical & Art Activities

### 1. Be a Gardener (Science/STEM)
Have students plant seeds in egg box holders. Discuss what they did, what they expect to happen, and steps needed to ensure seedlings grow.

### 2. Heads and Bodies (Art/Language)
Each student draws a head, folds paper, passes it. Next student draws body, folds, passes. Continue with feet, then names. Unfold to reveal funny characters and describe them.

### 3. Who has the Coin? (Math/Logic)
Two teams face each other. Team A passes a coin secretly. Team captain of B gives commands (crawl, turn over, flat hands) to detect who has the coin.

### 4. Shadow Plays (Drama/Language)
Hang sheet in front of lamp. Teams act out scenes as shadows, others guess the scene.

### 5. Mirror Writing (Language/Art)
Students write reversed letters that can only be read in a mirror. Swap and try to read without mirrors first.

### 6. Paper Fortune Teller (Math/Craft)
Fold paper into fortune teller. Write 8 fortunes. Students choose numbers to reveal fortunes.

### 7. Beetle Drive Game (Math/Art)
Roll dice to draw animal parts. Create custom versions with different animals.

### 8. Skipping Games (PE/Language)
Rope turners give commands while student jumps (clap hands, touch nose, etc.).

### 9. Paper Clothes (Art/Design)
Teams use newspapers and tape/staples to dress team members following instructions.

### 10. Create a Library (Language/Organization)
Categorize books, practice alphabetical ordering, explain categorization choices.

### 11. Tell the Time (Math)
Make moveable clock faces from paper. Practice asking/telling time in pairs.

### 12. Funny Shopping (Language)
Cross out nouns in news articles. Reader pauses at blanks, "shopkeeper" fills with shop items.

### 13. Traditional Remedies (Science/Social Studies)
Groups discuss and present traditional remedies for various ailments.

### 14. What can I find at the Beach? (Vocabulary)
Teams list items found at specific locations (beach, supermarket, hospital, etc.).

### 15. Invisible Writing (Science)
Write with lemon juice, messages appear when held over heat source.

### 16. Alphabet Drawings (Art/Language)
Turn letters into creative drawings where the letter is integral to the image.

### 17. Book Review (Language)
Two-minute reviews about recently read books.

### 18. Breaking News (Language/Drama)
Groups create fictitious events and deliver as news bulletins.

### 19. Rampage of Appreciation (Social-Emotional)
Write things you're thankful for on post-it notes, share on board.

### 20. Dream Team (Sports/Logic)
Create dream sports teams and justify selections.

### 21. Where Can I...? (Vocabulary/Geography)
Compile questions about where to do things, quiz classmates.

### 22. Perfect Neighbour (Social Studies)
List top 5 qualities of good and bad neighbors.

### 23. Run to... (Listening/PE)
Teacher describes area of room, students run to correct location.

### 24. Talking Pets (Creative Writing)
Imagine what pets would ask/tell owners if they could talk.

### 25. Incentives (Social Studies/Economics)
Brainstorm workplace incentives and which are most beneficial.

### 26. New Year Resolutions (Social-Emotional)
Discuss common resolutions, kept and broken ones.

### 27. Debate Social Networking (Language/Technology)
Teams debate pros and cons of social networking.

### 28. Historical Nursery Rhymes (History/Language)
Match nursery rhymes to their historical origins (e.g., Humpty Dumpty = cannon, Grand Old Duke of York = Battle of Wakefield).

## Subject-Specific Game Ideas

### Math Games
- Number bingo (1-20, 1-100)
- Math relay races
- Shape hunts
- Counting songs
- Dice addition/subtraction
- Fraction pizza making
- Measurement Olympics

### Language/Reading Games
- Word bingo
- Spelling bee
- Story chain
- Rhyme time
- Vocabulary scavenger hunt
- Grammar relay

### Science/STEM Games
- Nature scavenger hunts
- Simple experiments
- Classification games
- Life cycle sequencing
- Weather station activities

### Social Studies Games
- Map treasure hunts
- Timeline ordering
- Culture show and tell
- Community helper matching

### Art & Music Games
- Color mixing experiments
- Rhythm patterns
- Instrument identification
- Art style matching
