Getting an AdSense rejection for "Low value content" means Google’s automated review determined your site doesn't yet provide enough original, unique, or high-quality information to be valuable to advertisers. [1, 2] 
Why This Happened
This specific violation usually occurs if your site has:

* Thin Content: Pages with very little text, mostly images, or videos without accompanying original analysis.
* Lack of Originality: Content that is copied from other sites, heavily rephrased ("spun"), or provides no unique insights compared to existing sources.
* AI-Generated Material: Content produced entirely by AI without human editing or expertise often gets flagged.
* Navigation Issues: Broken links, empty categories, or a site that feels "under construction". [3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8] 

------------------------------
How to Fix It
Before clicking "Request review," you should perform a thorough audit of your site:

| Task [4, 5, 6, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12] | Goal |
|---|---|
| Audit Content Depth | Aim for at least 20–30 high-quality posts. Articles should ideally be 800–1,200+ words to show depth. |
| Add Originality | Ensure every post includes your own expertise, unique perspectives, or personal case studies. Avoid just "re-newsing" what's already out there. |
| Fix "Thin" Pages | Identify and either expand or delete pages with very little text. This includes empty category or tag pages. |
| Improve Site Layout | Use a clean, mobile-responsive theme. Ensure you have essential pages: About Us, Contact Us, Privacy Policy, and Terms & Conditions. |
| Verify Indexing | Check Google Search Console to ensure your articles are actually indexed. If Google can't find your content, it can't value it. |

Next Steps

   1. Stop applying immediately. Repeated rejections without changes can lead to longer wait times.
   2. Spend 15–20 days actively adding new, original content and fixing site structure.
   3. Once fixed, check the "I confirm I have fixed the issues" box in AdSense and click Request review. [13]
