Yes, you can absolutely use one VPS to host two (or even dozens) of websites. In fact, this is how most professional developers manage their projects to keep costs down and stay independent.
Instead of paying for separate hosting plans, you use a single "box" and tell it how to route traffic based on the domain name being requested.
### How It Works: "Virtual Hosts" or "Server Blocks"
When a visitor types in websiteA.com, their request hits your VPS IP address. Your web server software (like **Nginx** or **Apache**) looks at the request header, sees the domain name, and directs the user to the specific folder on your server where that website's files live.
### The 3 Steps to Independence
If you decide to move away from Replit and host yourself, here is the high-level workflow:
 1. **Point your Domains:** In Cloudflare, you would point the A-records for both brightboardapp.com and your second domain (e.g., theoptions.app) to the **same IP address** of your new VPS.
 2. **Organize your Folders:** Inside your VPS, you create separate folders for each site:
   * /var/www/brightboard/
   * /var/www/theoptions/
 3. **Configure the Web Server:** You create two small configuration files.
   * **File 1 says:** "If someone asks for brightboardapp.com, show them files from /var/www/brightboard/."
   * **File 2 says:** "If someone asks for theoptions.app, show them files from /var/www/theoptions/."
### Benefits of this Setup
 * **Zero "Hostage" Fees:** You aren't paying per-site "Pro" fees to Replit. You pay one flat rate for the VPS.
 * **Total SSL Control:** You can use a tool called **Certbot** (free) to handle all your SSL certificates directly on the VPS. No more "handshake" fights between Cloudflare and your host.
 * **Resource Sharing:** If one site is quiet, the other site can use all the server's RAM and CPU.
### Is there a catch?
The only "catch" is that you become your own IT manager. You'll need to handle the initial setup and basic security updates. However, since you are already managing Cloudflare DNS and Replit deployment, you already have the foundational skills needed to do this.
