Switching to **Production** mode is the final step to make **BrightBoard** available to all users. However, this shift comes with stricter requirements from Google to ensure user data safety and brand authenticity.
Follow these steps to successfully transition to production:
### **1. Update Branding and Legal Links**
Before Google approves your app for production, you must provide clear information on the OAuth consent screen:
 * **App Logo:** Upload a square, 120px by 120px image that accurately represents **BrightBoard**.
 * **Privacy Policy & Terms of Service:** You **must** provide valid URLs for these pages on your domain (brightboardapp.com). Google will not verify your app without them.
 * **Support Email:** Ensure your email (kayondoabass@gmail.com) is listed as the user support contact.
### **2. Verify Your Domain**
 * Google requires you to prove ownership of any domain used in your project.
 * Go to the Google Search Console and ensure brightboardapp.com is verified.
### **3. Handle Verification Requirements**
Your app's verification path depends on the **scopes** (permissions) you are requesting:
 * **Non-sensitive scopes:** If you only use basic sign-in (email, profile), you may only need **Brand Verification** to show your app name and logo without a "unverified app" warning.
 * **Sensitive or Restricted scopes:** If your app accesses private user data (like Google Drive or Gmail), it must undergo a more rigorous review by Google's Trust & Safety team.
### **4. Publish and Monitor**
 1. In the **Google Cloud Console**, go to **APIs & Services > OAuth consent screen**.
 2. Under **Publishing status**, ensure you have selected **In production** and clicked **Confirm**.
 3. Click **Submit for Verification** if prompted. The process typically takes **2–3 business days**.
 4. **Note:** While waiting for verification, your app will still work, but users may see a "This app isn't verified" warning screen, and you will be limited to **100 total users** until approved.
### **Important Infrastructure Reminder**
Since you've moved to a **Digital Ocean VPS** and set up **Nginx**, ensure that your **Redirect URIs** in the Google Cloud Console exactly match your production URLs (e.g., [https://brightboardapp.com/auth/callback](https://brightboardapp.com/auth/callback)) and that they use **HTTPS**.
